Tiger Scheduling

Project Conception

Tiger Scheduling was a simple project I worked on to introduce myself to application development. I wanted to understand what goes into building mobile iOS applications and what the experience is like for developers who build this kind of software.

To start off, I decided to solve an issue that everyone, including myself, suffers from: simple forgetfulness. Whether it's forgetting to complete a task at work or simply forgetting to complete a daily errand, we all seem to forget the simple things.

The User Interface

While aiming to fix forgetfulness, I also wanted this app to have a simple, easy-to-use, interface so that users can accomplish their tasks without needing to navigate to any new pages. By having one main interface, all the user needed to do was input any information they deemed important and a specific time when they needed to be reminded without having to navigate 10 other pages to accomplish the task.

In case it wasnt obvious, the UI's design was inspired by my need for simplicity and with the theme of the university I called home for four years. Clemson University's purple on orange was a nice color combination and as well as a cute little chibi tiger to convey what the app is supposed to do.

A Simple App to Conquer the Day

Tiger Scheduling, while simple in design, allows it's users to plan out their week by allowing them to create quick reminders. Busy people already have their calendars full and information can get lost. By using this app, people can stay up to date with tasks in and out of the workplace without needing to navigate a complex UI.
